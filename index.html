McGill University Libraries Centraide 2023

The University’s 2023 Centraide campaign begins November 13. Celebrating its 50th year, Centraide of Greater Montreal supports a network of 375 agencies and joint projects every year.

When you give to Centraide, you help the lives of one in five people in Greater Montreal. The community organizations that Centraide supports provide direct aid to Montrealers in need, reducing social exclusion, supporting academic and social success, and improving conditions for those living in poverty.

There are many ways to give! The easiest way is through payroll deduction. Sign up by following the personalized link included in the emails you receive from the Centraide campaign to your McGill email account. Once on the Centraide website, select the payment option, “payroll deduction,” and specify the total amount you want to give.

All the Libraries’ activities this year are paid by Interac e-Transfer or cash except for the HQ game, which uses the Artez platform and accepts credit card payment. If paying by e-Transfer, please make separate transfers for each activity (e.g., one e-Transfer to buy raffle tickets, another e-Transfer to participate in the 50/50 lottery).

Activity #2: I’m thinking of a call number (50/50 lottery)

Open to McGill Libraries staff

This is a traditional 50/50 lottery but with a library-oriented twist: Instead of selecting lottery numbers, participants select Library of Congress call numbers from our collection!

For this activity, the universe of possible call numbers is somewhat artificial. We use our Circulation Item Inventory report from October 22, which does not include electronic resources, and we further filter it (imperfectly!) to arrive at a list of items with LC call numbers. Finally, we deduplicate it so a given call number only appears once.

Just like the raffle, the relationship between how much you donate and how many call numbers you can “play” is given by

$$c(d)=⌊√^8{d}^9+1/2d⌋$$

Activity #3: Pet photo and painting prints

Open to McGill Libraries staff

Interested in getting a beautiful glossy or satin print of your favourite pet? Print your pet photo as is or turn it into a watercolour painting, “poster edges” painting or an oil painting. Choose one of two options of finish: Glossy with deep black shades or satin for a soft contrast image. Under normal conditions, your prints will last between 25 and 50 years!

Price is \$10 per 8×10″ print or \$10 for three 5×7″ prints.

Activity #5: McGill Libraries HQ (10-second trivia game via Zoom)

Open to the McGill community

November is International Games Month. Before it comes to a close, let’s play our own version of HQ Trivia, the smartphone trivia game that seemed to take the world by storm six years ago.

The competition is open to the McGill community and will be played via Zoom November 27–30 from 12:00 to 12:10 each afternoon. Players are asked 10 multiple-choice questions in increasing order of difficulty and have just 10 seconds to answer each question. Everyone is welcome to play along, but only those who have donated are eligible to win the grand prize. Players’ cumulative score is calculated based on their $n$ highest-scoring days, where $n$ is equal to the number of E-Tickets purchased. An E-Ticket is \$4 and gets you entry to one game, so purchasing four E-Tickets and playing all four days is your best shot at winning the grand prize.

The participant with the highest score wins \$100 cash! (Rapidity of response will be used to break a tie if necessary.)

Zoom login details are now available. Please use your McGill email address as your Zoom name when you join the meeting. Note that you do not need a webcam or microphone to participate.

Purchase your E-Tickets on Centraide’s Artez platform. Deadline to purchase is December 1.
